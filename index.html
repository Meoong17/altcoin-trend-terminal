How to read this terminal

Every number below is also explained inline — hover any dotted label.

Trend Score (0–100)

A weighted technical composite per coin: breakout 30% (proximity to 30d high, RSI in the 55–68 momentum zone, volume expansion, ATR expansion), relative strength vs BTC 30%, trend consistency 20% (green-day ratio, EMA20/50 slope), volatility compression 10%, macro 10%. The small chips under the score are its drivers — each chip's number is that component's push above or below neutral, and they sum to score − 50.

It is a transparent heuristic, not a backtested model. Green ≥60, amber 40–60, red <40.

Macro strip

GLF summarizes global liquidity: central-bank balance sheets, US M2, Treasury account, reverse repo and the dollar, blended as z-scores. Repo Stress watches the SOFR−EFFR spread — the plumbing of dollar funding. Market Regime classifies BTC's state with fixed rules; altcoin longs historically work best in BULL TREND with GLF above 60.

Altcoin Season Index

The share of tracked coins beating BTC over 90 days. Above 75 = altcoin season; below 25 = bitcoin season. It measures breadth, not magnitude — a few giant winners can't fake it.

Volume block

14 daily bars of quote volume (closed candles only — the running day is excluded so mornings don't fake a dry-up). The tape below compares the last closed day to the 7-day average: >2.0× is a spike (breakout, news, capitulation), <0.5× means participation is drying up. "Building / fading" is the 3d vs 7d run-rate.

Entry Timing grade (A+ / A / B / Avoid)

A simplified letter grade for "is now a reasonable time to look at this coin" — derived from OTF (Opportunity & Timing), computed for every coin in the universe, not just DeFi/VaF ones. It answers a different question than trend score: trend score asks "is this trending," Entry Timing asks "is the setup actionable right now" (chart structure, strength vs BTC, position in its 30-day range).

Grade bands mirror VaF's own locked OTF tiers: A+ ≥41 (strong opportunity) · A ≥35 (good) · B ≥28 (watchlist/incomplete) · Avoid below that. Coverage shown alongside the badge tells you how many of the underlying metrics fed the grade — coins with a full VaF row can also have catalyst/supply timing filled in manually for a fuller picture. This grade is completely separate from trend score and VaF: it never changes either one, and neither changes it.

VaF row (DeFi & infra coins)

The Direct Value Accrual Framework scores whether the token — not just the protocol — captures value. PQ /50 protocol quality, NF /50 structural value capture (buybacks, burns, fee share measured from DeFiLlama holders-revenue), RG /50 repricing gap. VaF = PQ+NF+RG, out of 150; 105+ is strong, 120+ elite.

OTF /50 scores timing only and never changes VaF. VFR is the 30-day confirmation ratio: token-positive value ÷ dilution — N/A means dilution data hasn't been manually verified, never a guess. CONF A–D grades research reliability; machine-only rows cap at B and low data coverage degrades it.

The core rule the framework enforces: protocol revenue is not token revenue unless it reaches the token. A great protocol with no value route scores high PQ, low NF — and the verdict says so.

F-Score & Composite

F-Score is the simpler fundamental composite (TVL growth, revenue yield, fee acceleration, value-accrual share). Composite blends 60% technical + 40% fundamental — fundamentals select, technicals time.

Source badges

No badge = Binance primary. BYBIT (amber) = tier-2 fallback, full metrics intact. CS FALLBACK (red) = tier-3, price metrics only, volume history unavailable. MACRO DEGRADED banner = part of the macro layer is neutral-filled; trend scores still compute but their macro slice isn't live.

Honesty rules

Missing data is shown as n/a and its weight excluded — never silently zero-filled. Scores are unvalidated heuristics for research prioritization. Nothing here is trading advice.
